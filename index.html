Air Quality Compliance

Ensuring proper ventilation and air circulation.

Improper air quality can lead to respiratory issues and affect employee well-being. Ensure CO2 levels are regulated and that your office is well-ventilated.

  • Recommended Action: Regular air quality checks.
  • Best Practices: Install CO2 monitors and ensure HVAC systems are working properly.

Ergonomics Compliance

Creating comfortable and safe workspaces to avoid injuries.

Ensuring employees use ergonomically designed furniture can prevent long-term health problems such as back pain, wrist strain, etc.

  • Recommended Action: Provide ergonomic desks and chairs.
  • Best Practices: Educate employees on proper posture and workstation setup.

Noise Levels Compliance

Maintaining safe noise levels to prevent hearing loss.

Excessive noise in the workplace can lead to hearing loss or increased stress levels. It‚Äôs crucial to monitor sound levels regularly.

  • Recommended Action: Use soundproofing materials.
